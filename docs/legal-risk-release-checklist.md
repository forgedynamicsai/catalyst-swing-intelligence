# Legal / Regulatory Release Checklist

This checklist is a non-lawyer risk screen. It is not legal advice.

Use it before publishing a release, merging examples, or accepting contributor changes.
If in doubt about any item, consult a qualified legal or compliance professional
before proceeding.

---

## 1. Advice Boundary

- [ ] The project is framed as decision support, research structure, and trade journaling — not as financial, investment, legal, tax, or compliance advice.
- [ ] The project does not tell users to buy, sell, short, hold, or avoid any real security.
- [ ] The project does not tailor recommendations to a user's age, income, account size, risk tolerance, family situation, or financial goals.
- [ ] The project does not claim to be an investment adviser, broker-dealer, fiduciary, registered representative, or financial professional of any kind.
- [ ] No output from this skill creates or implies an adviser-client, broker-client, or fiduciary relationship.

---

## 2. Compensation Boundary

- [ ] The project does not offer paid personalized securities advice.
- [ ] The project does not include paid trade calls, paid Discord signals, paid newsletters, or private recommendation tiers.
- [ ] The project does not include affiliate brokerage links.
- [ ] The project does not promote a security, ETF, crypto token, issuer, or broker in exchange for any form of compensation.
- [ ] Any future sponsorship or affiliate relationship must be reviewed by a qualified professional before publication and clearly disclosed if permitted.

---

## 3. Claims Boundary

- [ ] The project does not claim to beat the market.
- [ ] The project does not promise alpha or excess returns.
- [ ] The project does not promise reduced losses.
- [ ] The project does not imply predictive accuracy.
- [ ] The project does not cherry-pick examples as proof of trading performance.
- [ ] The Crowd Signal Quality Score is not presented as an expected-return score, buy score, risk rating, or prediction of crowd accuracy.
- [ ] Signal trajectory interpolation is used only to classify the crowd signal lifecycle — not to forecast price, return, fair value, or downside risk.

---

## 4. Examples Boundary

- [ ] Examples are fictional or clearly historical/educational.
- [ ] Examples use placeholders: `TICKER_A`, `Company X`, `ETF_B`, `Sector Y`, `Catalyst Z`.
- [ ] Examples do not include current actionable entry/exit levels for real securities.
- [ ] Examples do not include the maintainer's current holdings, cost basis, unrealized gains, or account size.
- [ ] Examples do not imply that fictional outcomes represent typical or expected results.

---

## 5. Public-Affiliation Boundary

- [ ] The project does not use the maintainer's government title, agency name, official duties, diplomatic role, official position, or public office to market, promote, or lend credibility to the project.
- [ ] The project does not imply government endorsement, interagency collaboration, or official use.
- [ ] The project is created and maintained only with personal resources, on personal time, and without use of government resources.
- [ ] If the maintainer is subject to ethics regulations, outside-employment rules, financial-conflict rules, or public-integrity standards, those obligations have been reviewed and complied with before any public release.

---

## 6. Release Decision

A release should not ship if it creates the impression of:

- personalized investment advice
- a securities recommendation or signal service
- paid access to trade calls or premium research
- compensated securities promotion or issuer marketing
- guaranteed or superior performance
- government endorsement or official backing

When in doubt, hold the release and seek qualified legal review.
