# Example: crowd-scan

> This is a fictional example and not financial advice.
> All tickers, companies, themes, and scores are illustrative only.
> This example does not represent real market conditions or actual crowd signals.

---

## Input

```
Use crowd-scan to evaluate whether the crowd signal around Company X
and AI infrastructure demand is coherent and evidenced.
```

---

## Expected Output Shape

```markdown
## Crowd Signal Assessment

- Theme: AI infrastructure power demand
- Source universe: X/finTwit (accounts: @account_A, @account_B, independent analysts),
  Reddit r/investing, r/stocks; financial media (Publication Z)
- Core crowd claim: Power scarcity will create a durable earnings tailwind for
  companies with contracted power capacity serving AI data centers
- Mechanism: AI workload growth → rising data-center power consumption →
  supply constraint for companies without locked-in capacity → pricing power
  and margin expansion for contracted power providers
- Signal trajectory: Accelerating — independent mentions increasing across
  finance communities; not yet mainstream media
- Crowd Signal Quality Score: 68/100
  - Base: Signal volume 11/15, Source independence 16/20, Specificity 16/20,
    Evidence quality 13/20, Time acceleration 7/10, Catalyst alignment 8/10,
    Dissent quality 2/5
  - Raw base: 73/100
  - Penalties applied:
    - Meme/hype: −0 (mechanism cited, emotion low)
    - Crowding: −5 (moderately known in energy/utilities circles)
    - Price-moved: −0 (thesis-related names have not moved significantly)
    - Single-source: −0 (independent convergence confirmed)
    - Loose ticker basket: −0 (transmission direct for contracted providers)
  - Adjusted score: 68/100
- Evidence quality: analyst notes + earnings transcripts + power contract filings
- Source independence: independent — multiple unrelated accounts citing same mechanism
- Dissent quality: credible dissent exists — some analysts argue hyperscaler capex
  could slow, removing demand catalyst
- Priced-in risk: partially priced — power names have moved on the theme but
  contracted-capacity premium not fully reflected in current multiples

## Market Transmission

- Earnings impact: Direct for companies with locked-in power agreements; indirect
  for equipment/infrastructure suppliers
- Margin impact: Positive for contracted power providers; neutral for uncontracted
- Supply/demand impact: Power supply constraint → pricing power → EBITDA expansion
- Policy/regulatory impact: Permitting timelines could extend supply-side response;
  IRA credits may accelerate some capacity
- Multiple/risk-premium impact: If constraint persists, risk-premium compression
  possible for contracted names
- Catalyst timing: Next earnings cycle — management commentary on contract pipeline
  is the primary dated catalyst

## Trade Fit Classification

- Classification: watchlist
- Why: Signal quality is moderate-to-high (68/100), mechanism is specific, but
  crowding is increasing and some names have partially moved on the theme.
  Entry discipline analysis (beaten-down check, entry zone, catalyst date) is
  required before deployment.
- What separate analysis is still required:
  - Entry quality scoring for specific instruments
  - Concentration check if adding to existing sector exposure
  - Earnings-gate review before any binary catalyst hold
  - Thesis-broken conditions (what would invalidate the power-scarcity narrative)
- Not a buy/sell/hold recommendation.

## Rejected / Too Loose

- Uranium/nuclear ETF basket — thematic, but earnings transmission to AI power
  demand is indirect and delayed by years
- Broad utilities ETF — too diversified; contracted AI-power exposure is small
  fraction of holdings
- Crypto mining names — different power demand profile; listed as AI-adjacent but
  mechanism is not the same
```

---

## What This Example Demonstrates

1. The Crowd Signal Quality Score (68/100) measures signal evidence — not whether
   the user should buy anything.
2. Signal trajectory (Accelerating) describes the crowd narrative lifecycle —
   not a price forecast.
3. Trade Fit Classification (`watchlist`) is a signal-routing decision —
   not a buy recommendation.
4. Separate entry, valuation, and thesis analysis is explicitly required before
   any capital decision.
