# Example: publish-signal

> This is a fictional example and not financial advice.
> This example shows the required disclaimer structure for a compliant social post.
> Do not use this as a template for promoting real securities.

---

## Input

```
Use publish-signal to write a compliant X thread sharing the crowd-scan
analysis of the AI infrastructure power demand signal.
```

---

## Expected Output Shape

```markdown
1/3
Wisdom of the Crowds experiment: I assessed the crowd signal around AI data-center
power demand across X/finTwit and Reddit.

Signal quality score: 68/100. Trajectory: accelerating.
The mechanism: power scarcity → pricing power for contracted providers.

Here's what the crowd is saying — and what the evidence actually supports 🧵

---

2/3
Evidence quality: analyst notes + earnings transcripts + contract filings.
Source independence: confirmed — multiple unrelated accounts citing same mechanism.
Priced-in risk: partially priced.
Signal fit: monitor — requires entry and thesis analysis before any capital decision.

Loose signals rejected: broad uranium baskets and unrelated power names
without direct AI-load contract exposure.

---

3/3
⚠️ NOT financial advice. NOT a solicitation to buy or sell anything.
DYOR. I may or may not hold related positions.
This is a crowd-signal quality experiment, not a recommendation.

What signals are YOU tracking? Reply below.

#WisdomOfTheCrowds #MarketResearch #DecisionDiscipline
```

---

## Mandatory Disclaimer Requirements

Every `publish-signal` output must include all five:

1. "Not financial advice"
2. "Not a solicitation to buy or sell"
3. "DYOR" or "do your own research"
4. "I may or may not hold positions"
5. "This is an experiment / not a recommendation"

Missing any one of these is a quality failure.

---

## What This Example Demonstrates

1. The post shares a crowd-signal quality score — not a buy/sell/hold call.
2. The post explicitly separates evidence quality from trade fit.
3. All five required disclaimers are present.
4. The post invites discussion — not followers or trades.
5. Hashtags are neutral/educational — not promotional.
