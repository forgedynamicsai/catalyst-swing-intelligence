## Crowd Signal Quality Score Is Not a Buy Score

The Crowd Signal Quality Score (0–100) measures the quality and coherence of
a crowd signal — the volume, independence, specificity, evidence quality,
and trajectory of a market narrative.

It does not measure:

- expected return
- security safety or risk
- fair value or price target
- probability of trade success
- whether a user should buy, sell, or hold anything

A high Crowd Signal Quality Score means the signal is better evidenced.
It does not mean the security is cheap, safe, or likely to rise.

## Signal Trajectory Is Not a Price Forecast

Signal trajectory classification (Emerging / Accelerating / Mainstreaming /
Saturated / Fading) describes the lifecycle of a crowd narrative.

It does not forecast price direction, expected return, fair value,
downside risk, or any other outcome for any security.
